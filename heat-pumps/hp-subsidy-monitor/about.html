About HP Subsidy Monitor

An interactive dashboard providing a global overview of heat pump and hybrid heating markets across 32 European countries — with a strong focus on government subsidies, policy changes, energy prices, and market trends.

What This Dashboard Covers

Market Intelligence — Heat pump market size by country, annual sales volumes, growth trends, and key adoption indicators. Focus on hydronic systems (air-to-water, ground-source, exhaust-air, hybrids). Where data includes air-to-air units, this is explicitly labeled.

Policy & Subsidies — Overview of current subsidy programs by country: type and size of grants, tax credits, and incentives. Tracking of policy changes over time — introductions, increases, reductions, cancellations. Special attention to hybrid systems (heat pump + gas boiler) where applicable.

News & Signals — Latest news related to heat pumps, heating policies, and subsidies from 25 countries in local languages. AI-generated summaries of articles with tagging of key events: subsidy increases, policy rollbacks, new regulations, and signals that may impact the market.

Technology Scope

  • AW — Air-to-Water heat pumps (residential and commercial)
  • LW — Liquid-to-Water / Ground-source / Brine-to-Water
  • EW — Exhaust Air heat pumps (with water distribution)
  • Hybrids — Heat pump + gas boiler systems (add-on and integrated)

Excluded: Air-to-Air reversible heat pumps (split systems, VRF). Where market data includes A2A, this is clearly marked.

Data Pipeline

All data flows through an automated pipeline hosted on Vercel (serverless infrastructure, auto-scaling). The pipeline runs on a fixed schedule with no downtime required.

Energy prices are fetched directly from official statistical APIs — Eurostat (EU/EFTA), UK Department for Energy Security (DESNZ), and Swiss Federal Electricity Commission (ElCom). These are deterministic JSON/XLSX/SPARQL endpoints — no AI interpretation involved. Prices are converted to EUR using European Central Bank exchange rates via the Frankfurter API.

News & policy signals are collected from Google News RSS feeds in 25 languages across all tracked countries. Each article is summarized by Llama 3.3 70B (hosted by Groq, with automatic fallback to Llama 3.1 8B if quota is reached). The AI deduplicates — if multiple outlets cover the same event, they are merged into a single summary. Top 5 markets get up to 6 articles, next 6 markets up to 3, remaining countries 1 each.

Manufacturer news covers product launches, partnerships, and factory developments from major heat pump brands (Daikin, Bosch, Vaillant, Viessmann, NIBE, Mitsubishi, Panasonic, Samsung, and others). Collected daily from global news sources via the same RSS + AI pipeline, displayed on the dedicated Manufacturers tab.

Subsidy programs, market data, and policy outlook are researched by Google Gemini 2.5 Flash with built-in Google Search capability. Gemini reads official government portals and industry sources, extracts structured data (program names, maximum amounts with component breakdowns, annual sales figures), and returns source URLs for verification. A tiered trust model is applied: government sources are prioritized; secondary sources require higher confidence scores and smaller change thresholds. A year-forward rule ensures that newer data is never overwritten by older.

Validation checks applied to every update: source URL must exist and point to a credible domain (government, statistical office, or industry association); values must fall within predefined sanity bounds; large deviations from existing data require confirmation from a primary source; the scope is enforced — only hydronic heat pumps (AW, LW, EW, Hybrids), with air-to-air units explicitly excluded or labeled.

Storage & display: All validated data is written to a PostgreSQL database (Supabase, EU Frankfurt region). When you open any page, the browser fetches the latest data directly from this database in real-time. All pages read from the same source — numbers are identical everywhere. Source links (↗) are provided for independent verification.

Disclaimer

All information presented on this dashboard is sourced exclusively from publicly available, open-access internet sources — including government portals, official statistical agencies, industry associations, and public news outlets. No proprietary, paywalled, or restricted data is used.

This dashboard uses AI-assisted data collection and processing. While multiple validation checks are applied, errors are possible. Always verify critical figures via the source links (↗) provided on the dashboard before making financial or business decisions.
