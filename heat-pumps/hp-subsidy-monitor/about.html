About HP Subsidy Monitor

A comprehensive dashboard tracking heat pump subsidies, energy prices, and market data across 29 European countries.

Purpose

HP Subsidy Monitor provides a centralized view of government subsidy programs for heat pumps and hybrid heating systems across Europe. It helps industry professionals, policy analysts, and consumers understand the current subsidy landscape, track policy changes, and compare conditions across countries.

What We Track

Technology scope:

  • AW — Air-to-Water heat pumps (residential <20kW, commercial 20–350kW)
  • LW — Liquid-to-Water / Ground-source / Brine-to-Water
  • EW — Exhaust Air heat pumps (with water distribution)
  • Hybrids — Heat pump + gas boiler systems (add-on and integrated)

Excluded: Air-to-Air reversible heat pumps (split systems, VRF). Where market data includes A2A units, this is clearly marked.

Single source of truth: All energy price numbers across the dashboard, country pages, and the Electricity Prices page are read from the same Supabase energy_prices table, refreshed monthly by the cron job. The dashboard's "current price" stat shows the H1+H2 average for the latest year, identical everywhere.

Why monthly for prices: Eurostat publishes electricity & gas prices semi-annually (H1 in November, H2 in May/June). The cron checks on the 1st of each month — most months it's a no-op, but when Eurostat releases new data, the site updates within 24 hours, automatically.

Why manual approval for subsidies/market_size: No public API exists for these (EHPA reports are paywalled, government subsidy programs change unpredictably). Llama reads news daily and queues suggested updates with confidence scores. An admin reviews and approves, preventing AI hallucinations from corrupting the dataset.

Methodology

Energy prices: Latest Eurostat H1+H2 average (covering 2500–4999 kWh/year residential band, all taxes included, in EUR). For UK: gov.uk DESNZ "Domestic prices in the IEA" annual data, converted from pence/kWh to EUR via European Central Bank rates. For Switzerland: ElCom national median tariff for category H4 (typical 4500 kWh household), in CHF/kWh, converted via ECB.

Market data: National associations with hydronic-only data are preferred (BWP, Uniclima, SULPU, VWP, HPA, PORT PC, WPA, VPI, FWS). Where only total figures exist (incl. A2A), this is marked. Updates extracted from news articles via Llama, then human-approved.

HP penetration: cumulative installed base ÷ total households × 100%. Cumulative base updated when news articles cite EHPA cumulative figures (e.g., "Germany has 1.6M heat pumps installed").

Subsidies: Verified against official government portals. Max available amount shown. Updates detected from news via Llama, then human-approved before going live.
