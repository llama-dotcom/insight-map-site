About HP Subsidy Monitor

An interactive dashboard providing a global overview of heat pump and hybrid heating markets across 32 European countries — with a strong focus on government subsidies, policy changes, energy prices, and market trends.

What This Dashboard Covers

Market Intelligence — Heat pump market size by country, annual sales volumes, growth trends, and key adoption indicators. Focus on hydronic systems (air-to-water, ground-source, exhaust-air, hybrids). Where data includes air-to-air units, this is explicitly labeled.

Policy & Subsidies — Overview of current subsidy programs by country: type and size of grants, tax credits, and incentives. Tracking of policy changes over time — introductions, increases, reductions, cancellations. Special attention to hybrid systems (heat pump + gas boiler) where applicable.

News & Signals — Latest news related to heat pumps, heating policies, and subsidies from 25 countries in local languages. AI-generated summaries of articles with tagging of key events: subsidy increases, policy rollbacks, new regulations, and signals that may impact the market.

Technology Scope

  • AW — Air-to-Water heat pumps (residential and commercial)
  • LW — Liquid-to-Water / Ground-source / Brine-to-Water
  • EW — Exhaust Air heat pumps (with water distribution)
  • Hybrids — Heat pump + gas boiler systems (add-on and integrated)

Excluded: Air-to-Air reversible heat pumps (split systems, VRF). Where market data includes A2A, this is clearly marked.

How Updates Work

Data is refreshed automatically on a fixed schedule — no manual input required. Energy prices are pulled directly from official statistical databases (Eurostat, gov.uk, ElCom) using their public APIs. Subsidy and market data is researched monthly via AI-powered web search of official government and industry sources. Every update passes validation checks: newer data cannot be overwritten by older, values must fall within reasonable bounds, and government sources are prioritized over secondary. All numbers on the dashboard include a source link (↗) for independent verification.

Currency conversions (GBP, CHF → EUR) use official ECB exchange rates via the Frankfurter API.
