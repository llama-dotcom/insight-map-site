About HP Subsidy Monitor

A comprehensive dashboard tracking heat pump subsidies, energy prices, and market data across 29 European countries.

Purpose

HP Subsidy Monitor provides a centralized view of government subsidy programs for heat pumps and hybrid heating systems across Europe. It helps industry professionals, policy analysts, and consumers understand the current subsidy landscape, track policy changes, and compare conditions across countries.

What We Track

Technology scope:

  • AW — Air-to-Water heat pumps (residential <20kW, commercial 20–350kW)
  • LW — Liquid-to-Water / Ground-source / Brine-to-Water
  • EW — Exhaust Air heat pumps (with water distribution)
  • Hybrids — Heat pump + gas boiler systems (add-on and integrated)

Excluded: Air-to-Air reversible heat pumps (split systems, VRF). Where market data includes A2A units, this is clearly marked as "incl. A2A".

Methodology

Market data: Where national associations provide hydronic-only breakdowns, those figures are used (BWP for Germany, Uniclima for France, SULPU for Finland, VWP for Netherlands, HPA for UK, PORT PC for Poland, WPA for Austria, VPI for Denmark, FWS for Switzerland). Where only total figures are available (including A2A), this is marked accordingly.

HP Penetration: Calculated as: installed heat pump base ÷ total number of households × 1,000. Source for installed base: EHPA or national statistics. Source for households: Eurostat. Where installed base is unavailable, shown as "N/A".

Energy prices: Residential electricity and gas prices. EU countries: Eurostat (half-yearly publication). UK: Ofgem price cap. Non-EU: GlobalPetrolPrices or national statistics.

Subsidies: Verified against official government portals. Maximum available subsidy amount shown (may require meeting all conditions). Programs marked as "active", "expiring", or "announced".

Data Quality & Transparency

  • Every data point has a named source
  • Countries where A2A cannot be excluded are marked "incl. A2A"
  • Countries with no reliable unit data show "N/A" instead of estimates
  • All subsidy programs link to official government pages
  • Methodology and sources are publicly documented
